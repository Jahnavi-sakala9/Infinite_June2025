Ratan Tata

Ratan Naval Tata was an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and visionary leader who served as the chairman of Tata Group from 1991 to 2012. Under his leadership, Tata Group transformed into a global powerhouse, acquiring brands like Jaguar Land Rover, Tetley Tea, and Corus Steel. Known for his humility, ethics, and commitment to social good, Ratan Tata left an indelible mark on Indian industry and society.

Achievements

  • Led Tata Group’s global expansion through major acquisitions like Jaguar Land Rover and Tetley Tea.
  • Launched the Tata Nano, the world’s most affordable car, aimed at empowering middle-class families.
  • Received India’s highest civilian honors: Padma Bhushan (2000) and Padma Vibhushan (2008).
  • Invested in over 40 startups, supporting innovation and entrepreneurship in India.
  • Promoted ethical business practices and corporate philanthropy through Tata Trusts.
  • Served on boards of global companies including Rolls Royce, Alcoa, and Mitsubishi.
  • Honored internationally with awards like the Honorary Knight Grand Cross (UK) and Order of Australia.
